How it works

Scan. Sip. See you soon.

STEP 1 — SCAN

A small card on the table

The card points your guest to a quick check-in page made for your place. First time here? We learn who took care of them. Been here before? We log the visit. Either way, they’re on your list and in this month’s drawing.

STEP 2 — SIP

The night carries on

Your guest scans, checks in, and goes right back to the glass, the conversation, the reason they came. No app. No pitch. No staff script. Like Old Friends takes it from there.

STEP 3 — SEE YOU SOON

A text that sounds like you

Days later, a warm note arrives from your place: personal, specific, and written in your voice. Not a coupon blast. An invitation back.

The promise

You do nothing. We mean it.

No portalNo dashboardNo staff scriptNo hardwareNo coupon blasts

People come back to places where somebody knows them. We built Like Old Friends around that one true thing. We set it up, write the notes, send them in your voice, and once a month, give you one simple report: who came back, why, and what it was worth.

Staff shout-outs

Guests tell us who took care of them, and the follow-up remembers. A bartender’s name. A server’s recommendation. A familiar detail from the night. That’s the Cheers factor, working quietly for you.

Google reviews, grown monthly

We ask the right guests at the right moment to say publicly what they already said at the table. No begging. No awkward QR-code plea. Just well-timed appreciation.

Slow-night rescue

When a Tuesday needs help, a pre-approved note goes to nearby regulars. Not the whole list. Not tourists who already left town. Just people close enough, familiar enough, and likely enough to come in.

Locals and visitors, sorted

Savannah nights are two audiences. We know which is which, so a visitor gets a warm memory and a local gets a reason to return next week.

The monthly basket

Every month, we put together a little thank-you from the neighborhood. Every signup is an entry, every check-in earns another, and every drawing gives people one more reason to come back.

Friends of friends

Coming soon: warm endorsements between neighboring businesses that actually like each other. Your guests hear it from you. Their guests hear it from them. Nobody swaps lists. Nobody gets spammed.
